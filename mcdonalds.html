Creating Space for Conversation in a Fast-food Restaurant

In fast-food, speed is king, and the opportunity to build connections at the counter gives way to efficiency. In my conversational UX class, my team built a chatbot that creates space for customers to connect with McDonald's brand while ordering.

TAKEAWAY #1

Test Utterances Early and Often

Building a chatbot that feels human requires a rich dataset and a deep understanding of how customers want to interact with it. Testing early and often generates both the knowledge and the data.

DEFINING INTENTS

Misplaced Focus

While conducting initial research, we extracted intents from the McDonald's mobile app and read online threads about people's orders online. Our research approach inadvertently limited the scope of our understanding to users that already decided what to order.

WRITING UTTERANCES

Too Specific

When translating user intents to utterances, we assumed people were mainly familiar with the McDonaldâ€™s menu and the app. Early version of utterances focused on requesting on specific menu items.

TESTING

A Collaborative Conversation

During our first round of user testing, we learned that people wanted to converse with the agent to help make decisions. To get suggestions. We rewrote our user utterances Continually testing helped build our utterances bank.

I have a sweet tooth

TAKEAWAY #2

Double Down on Brand Values

The agent should reflect the beloved McDonald's brand. Using the company brand guidelines and analyzing the existing language used in the McDonald's app, I defined the agent's personality as unpretentious, lighthearted, and approachable.

Unpretentious

Stay casual but use clear diction. Mcdonald's is loved across cultures, so inclusion is a priority.

Lighthearted

Use playful language to affirm choices and create positive, memorable interactions with the customer.

Approachable

Always be ready to help and never judge. Offer suggestions to help guide the customer.
WRITING

Adding Positive Reinforcements

The current flow requires users to click into podcast shows to access show information. The new design brings relevant and personalized AI-generated podcast information out from the podcast show page into the explore page.

DESIGN

A Card Carousel Accommodates Relevant Information

A carousel allows users to access more information quickly if they're interested and skip past podcasts if they're not. I used paper prototype testing to arrive at the final card layout.

FUTURE CONSIDERATIONS

Creators Deserve a Say in Podcast Picks

In the challenge, our scope was limited to the listener's experience. Given more time, Meng and I would have explored how creators would respond to AI representing them. We would explore creator tools to review and refine AI-generated descriptions.

REFLECTIONS

Lessons in Moving Foward

With only 4 weeks to complete the design challenge from prompt to presentation, we outlined each phase of the project. Inevitably, our design process wasn't linear but we were always able to move forward with each conversation.

Getting Unstuck

Our mentor helped us reframe when we got stuck by reminding us of the bigger picture. I learned to refer back to a Northstar to guide our decisions when stuck.

Prepardness Pays

Preparing objectives, questions, and a weekly progress summary for meetings allowed us to make the most of our mentor's time.
