THE CHALLENGE

Creating Space for Conversation in a Fast-food Restaurant

In fast-food, speed is king, and the opportunity to build connections at the counter gives way to efficiency. In my conversational UX class, my team built a chatbot that creates space for customers to connect with the McDonald's brand while ordering.

TAKEAWAY #1

Test Utterances Early and Often

Building a chatbot that feels human requires a rich dataset and a deep understanding of how customers want to interact with it. Testing early and often generates both the knowledge and the data.

DEFINING INTENTS

Misplaced Focus on Placing Orders

While conducting initial research, we extracted intents from the McDonald's mobile app and read social media threads on people's go to McDonald's orders. Our research approach inadvertently limited the scope of our understanding to users that already know what they want to order.

WRITING UTTERANCES

TESTING

Create a Collaborative Conversation

During our first round of user testing, we learned that people wanted to converse with the agent to help make decisions. We rewrote our user utterances to be more open to vague phrases that the lead the conversation to suggestions. Then, we continually tested these utterances to build our phrase bank (right).

TAKEAWAY #2

Stay True to Brand Values

The chat agent should embody the beloved McDonald's brand. From brand guidelines and analyzing the tone used in the McDonald's app, I crafted the agent's personality to be unpretentious, lighthearted, and approachable.

Unpretentious

Stay casual but use clear diction. Mcdonald's is loved across cultures, so inclusion is a priority.

Nice! Our fries are iconic.

Lighthearted

Playful language to affirms choices and create positive, memorable interactions.

Ooo chocolate chip is my favorite cookie!

Approachable

Always be ready to help and never judge. Offer suggestions to help guide the customer.

How can I help you today? Check out deals. See the menu.

TAKEAWAY #3

Don‚Äôt Let Technical Skill Limit Design

At the start of the project, Pete and I got caught up in figuring out how to customize each order item and replicating the menu, we ended up building our chat to only accept one item at a time instead of how people naturally want to order food.

BUILDING VUI

Adding One Item at a Time

We mirrored the functionality of the McDonald‚Äôs app, where users add one item to the cart at a time. This works well for users who decide as they browse, but not for those who already know their order.

VOICEFLOW PROTOTYPE

From Menu to Check Out

Our final design retained the flow of adding one item at a time.

FUTURE CONDSIDERATIONS

Ordering Multiple Items Matches Mental Models

Repeat McDonald's customers prefer to order multiple items at once, so if we had more time, we would implement that functionality in the future. I learned for conversational UX projects to use the mobile app to extract intents but don't try to mimic the functionality of the mobile app.
