Goal of the Game

The game is split into 3 types of players: Residents, Friends and Foes. The goal of the Foes are to collect 18+ victory points. The goal of the Residents and Friends are to defeat all the Foes.

Selecting Characters

Put the following in the role deck:
  • 1 burglar
  • Any remaining Foe according to the above chart
  • Residents as according to the chart above
  • Friends according to the chart above

Shuffle the role deck and pass out the roles to the players. Residents reveal their role,pick their characters, and get their players reference mats accordingly. Intruders do not get to pick their characters and cannot reveal whether they are a friend or a foe. All intruders begin with a generic intruder player reference mat. Once revealed, they swap out for their respective player reference mat.

Intruders

Intruders begin with a generic skill set and need to hold onto their role card. They are to act according to their game goals. Once an intruder’s identity is revealed, they must begin using their assigned role card for their skill sets. Intruders may reveal whether they are friend or foe by the following actions:

  • A player in the same room may confront the intruder by doing an awareness roll and if they roll 2 or higher then they may reveal the intruder’s identity. If the player fails to confront, they must end their turn immediately. If the player in turn is an unidentified intruder, they must reveal their identity.
  • A player in the same room may do a blind attack. During the attack, the identity is revealed and the intruder uses their assigned character card for their strength roll. If the intruder loses and is a friend, they must lose a skill point of their choosing.
  • The intruder may choose to attack anyone else. They must use the generic strength to do their strength roll. Their identity is revealed after the attack.
  • When an intruder walks into a room with the light on, their identity is revealed.

When the intruder is revealed, they transfer their skill increases and decreases they earned from their previous gameplay as a generic intruder. For example, if they have +1 speed and -2 strength from their starting position before they were revealed, then they would apply the same to their revealed character.

Placing Valuable items

Shuffle all valuable item cards in a deck. Roll one die to determine how many valuables will be in one bedroom. Draw the number of valuable item cards according to the die and place in the bedroom accordingly. Repeat for each bedroom, then place the remaining valuable items in the living room.

Character starting positions

Residents may choose to start at any bedroom or the living room as their starting position. Residents begin the game sleeping and must roll a 1 or higher awareness to wake up. If they roll a zero, they must pass their turn. You can be awakened if someone attacks you. Intruders begin the game in the street. They can enter the front yard freely, but the front door is locked. They must break open the front door with a strength roll of 3+. Once the front door is broken, any sleeping residents in the living room wake up, and the front door remains open for the rest of the game.

Every Turn

  1. Grip Roll

    At the start of each turn, the player must do a grip roll if they have a valuable item. A grip of below the number indicated on the card and the player must drop the item in the room they’re in.

  2. Chose one action: Use speed, draw chance card, or sleep

    • Use Speed

      Move the number of speed indicated on your card. You may also use 1 speed to lock/unlock a door or turn on the lights.

    • Draw a chance card

      Chance cards may help you or hurt you. There are several types: Companion cards, Influence card, and Story cards.

    • Sleep

      In the following turns, you must roll an awareness of 2 or higher to wake up or else you pass your turn.

  3. Do any of the interactions once in the room you end up in

    • Pick up a weapon or valuable item

      To pick up a weapon, drop the weapon the player is holding (if any) and pick up the weapon. To pick up a valuable item, drop the valuable item the player is currently holding (if any) in the current room and do an awareness roll. If the awareness is below 2 then the player may not pick up any items in the room.

    • Drop a companion or tool

      After dropping, any other player in the room can pick it up on their next turn.

    • Confront

      Use an awareness roll to reveal the identity of an intruder. An awareness of 2+ reveals the identity. If the player fails, they must end their turn.

    • Attack

      Use a strength roll to attack another player in the same room. If the attacker has the higher roll, they may do one of the following:
      • Steal a valuable item from the opponent. The loser must do a grip roll. A grip roll of 3+ allows the loser to hold onto the item. Below 2, and item is surrendered.
      • Steal a weapon from the opponent.
      • Steal a companion.
      • Reduce any one skill by one.
      • If the opponent is the food delivery, eat a food delivery order and heal yourself by 1 skill.
      • Put your opponent to sleep for one turn without healing. They keep their items or weapons.

How to Earn Victory Points

Foes can earn victory points by dropping items in the street. Victory points are the numbers in the yellow circle. The number in the green circle is the required grip roll needed to hold onto an item. Once dropped in the street, residents and friends cannot pick up valuable items.

How to Defeat Characters

A character is defeated when one of their skills falls below one.
