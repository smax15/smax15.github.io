Challenge

Redesign a dashboard to remotely monitor patients for cancer drug trials

For this home treatment framework, patients will have telehealth visit with their study physician before each cycle infusion. However, during normal telehealth visits the physicianâ€™s ability to assess a patient is limited by the format of teleconferencing. To make better use of the telehealth visits, remote monitoring is used to assist the physician in assessing the patient. The patient during the trial will use a combination of wearables and ePROs to provide the physician additional context. The problem I tackled was to update an existing remote monitoring web app to add new data types and enable a physician to assess a assess a patient remotely.

Secretely Frustrated

Study coordinators expressed that the study level view was easy to use and identify non compliant patients. However, through our conversations, the coordinators revealed they were using the patient-level data tables to aggregate data for reports, which is an unintended use of the monitoring dashboard.

PAINPOINTS

Add Features for Compiling Reports

They expressed the need to access data from the entire tracking period using the data tables to put together these reports. I identified 3 major opportunities for improvements:

1. Add the ability to edit tracking dates in app

2. Add a Date Picker to Access All Data

Only accessing data from 14 days made reports difficult to compile

3. Create both overview and detailed view of data
