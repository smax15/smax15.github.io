STUDY COORDINATOR DASHBOARD

Secretely Frustrated

Through the feedback sessions, the coordinators revealed they were using the previous dashboard to aggregate data for reports, which is an unintended use. They were endlessly frustrated that they could view data from the start of the trial or the current week. This drove my decision to increase data accessibility as a main focus of the redesign.

PAINPOINTS

Add Features for Compiling Reports

Most of the painpoints from the original dashboard were related to constraints on accessing data and controlling tracking dates. 3 major opportunities for improvements were:

1. Add the ability to edit tracking dates in app

Users didnâ€™t want to email the software team to edit tracking dates

2. Add a Date Picker to Access All Data

Only accessing data from 14 days made reports difficult to compile

3. Create both overview and detailed view of data

Users found the monitoring data didnâ€™t align with report data needs

REDESIGN

Toggle for Monitoring vs Reporting

Most of the time, the study coordinators will be using the dashboard to monitor the patients' compliance; the overview focuses on identifing days with missing data. Then, for report compiling, the detailed view gives access to data values.

PHYSICIAN DASHBOARD

The Doctor-Designer

At the start, the physician for this study had a unwavering vision for the dashboard in his head. I wireframed his design as a starting point to visualize the telehealth dashboard. The wireframe illustrated that the original vision's requirements caused the adverse events table to be unreadable.

DESIGN ITERATION

Focus on Primary Assessments

Further discussions revealed that the key information needed to assess vital signs were if they deviated from baseline so graphs were removed and alerts were used for measurements that needed attention. As a result adverse events has more real estate for create a readable table.

REFLECTIONS

Gently Push and Pull

In a hospital, the workplace hierarchy shapes behavior and certain voices are overshadowed or amplified. I learned two important lessons to get the most out of getting feedback from different stakeholders.

Pull Feedback

Study coordinators didn't mentioned their frustration with creating reports until I started interviewing them, despite working closely with them. I learned to create space for product feedback to understand user behavior.

Pushing My Expertise

Visual demonstrations is one of the best ways to justify design decisions that might not align with opinionated stateholders' original expectations.
